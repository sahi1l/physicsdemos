Equilibrium

Goal: Adjust the third force so that the ruler is in equilibrium.
Drag the red arrow or number...
  • ...left and right to position the force.
  • ...up and down to change the magnitude of the force.
  • ...to the other side of the ruler to change the direction of the force.
When you think the ruler is in equilibrium,
press the "Check answer" button.

Help

  • The ruler is in equilibrium when the net force and the net torque on the ruler are zero.
  • The torque exerted by a force is its magnitude times the lever arm measured from a pivot point. It points either clockwise or counterclockwise.
  • You can choose the pivot point to be wherever you like:

    Whether we choose a pivot on the left side of the ruler or in the middle, the torques still cancel.
