Kids Analogy

This is a very simplified model of what goes on inside a conductor or an insulator.
The are positive atomic nuclei (plus the non-valence electrons).
The are negative valence electrons; we're assuming there's one per atom.
If you click on the Insulator button, the electrons are forced to stay close to their own nucleus, like kids who have to stay in their own backyards.
If you click on the Conductor button, the electrons are allowed to roam everywhere inside the object, like kids who can roam the neighborhood.
Clicking on one of the long blue bars will extend it, and the electrons will react by being attracted to it. In a conductor, the "kids" can all run over to the edge of the material to look at the positive charge. In an insulator, the "kids" stay in their own yard, but get as close to the external charge as they can.
In both cases, notice that there is a layer of negative charge closest to the external source, and a layer of positive charge (due to the absence of electrons) on the far side. The block has become polarized.
